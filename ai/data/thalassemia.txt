THALASSEMIA KNOWLEDGE BASE

Thalassemia is a hereditary blood disorder.

Patients with thalassemia major often require regular blood transfusions every 2 to 5 weeks.

Iron overload is a common complication of repeated transfusions.

Iron chelation therapy may be required to remove excess iron from the body.

Common symptoms include fatigue, weakness, pale skin, and delayed growth.

The BloodBridge platform is designed to help connect thalassemia patients with donors and blood banks.
