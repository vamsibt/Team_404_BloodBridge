Blood donors must wait at least 56 days between whole blood donations.

People with fever should wait until they are completely recovered before donating blood.

Individuals with low hemoglobin levels may not be eligible to donate blood.

Thalassemia is a genetic blood disorder that often requires regular blood transfusions.

Blood donation helps save lives and supports patients who require transfusions.

O negative blood is considered a universal donor type.

AB positive blood is considered a universal recipient type.
